Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 28, 2026

Welcome to OpenStream. Your privacy is not a featureâ€”it is our foundation. This policy explains how we handle your data (spoiler: we mostly don't).

The TL;DR: OpenStream does not collect, store, or sell your personal data. There are no accounts, no tracking cookies, and no analytics.

1. Data Collection

OpenStream is a decentralized podcast player. We do not require you to create an account, provide an email address, or link any social media profiles. We collect zero personal information.

2. Local Data Storage

Any data generated through your use of the app stays on your device. This includes:

  • Your podcast subscriptions.
  • Your playback progress and history.
  • Downloaded audio files.
  • Your search history.

This data is stored in a local database (Room DB) and is never uploaded to our servers.

3. Third-Party Services

To provide you with podcast content, the app interacts with the following services:

  • iTunes Search API: Used to search the global podcast directory. No user identifiers are sent with these requests.
  • RSS Feeds: When you play or subscribe to a show, the app connects directly to the podcast creator's host server to fetch audio and metadata.

4. Permissions

The app requests only the permissions necessary for core functionality:

  • Internet: To stream audio and fetch show details.
  • Notifications: To allow you to control playback from your lock screen.

5. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally to reflect changes in the app. However, our commitment to "local-first" data will remain unchanged.

6. Contact Us

If you have questions about this privacy policy, you can reach out via our GitHub repository.
